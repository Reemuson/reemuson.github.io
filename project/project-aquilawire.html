Aquilawire VS.807

Introduction

Aquilawire is a heavily modified Voxelab Aquila X2 converted to CoreXZ akin to the Voron Switchwire.

It was bought for cheap on a black-friday sale and served me will with slight modification for many months. Until I discovered and fell into the world of Voron…

The switchwire provides linear rails, CoreXZ motion, kicking the Z screws to the curb, and saying goodbye to the hassle of dealing with z-wobble. It was like a laundry list of upgrades to boost speed, precision, and overall print quality.

I stumbled upon this gem of a project by boubounokefalos. They took the Aquila X2/Ender 3 frame and turned it into the Switchwire’s cousin. I self-sourced all of the parts needed to build this machine and printed the necessary parts & tweaked a few in ABS.

But, of course, the modding journey doesn’t stop there. Automatic z-offset became the next big thing on my list. Needed a nozzle end-stop for that, and I wasn’t about to wait around for parts to ship. I fired up the lathe and turned out the needed bushing and pin.

I then came across the Klicky probe by jlas1 - a game-changer. I opted for the KlickyPCB derivation as initial use of the KlickyNG seemed to be unreliable, I’ll chalk that up to poor initial tolerances on my printer. However, this became the key to hassle-free bed-levelling and nozzle swaps. Whipped up some configuration files in Klipper for auto-offset calibration. Now, I can switch nozzles easily, no feeler gauge required.

Now this printer takes it all in stride. Cruising at 250mm/s² external walls with 10k acceleration - it’s a beast. Prints PLA, ABS, PETG, TPU. Albiet with lots of testing to find the right printer settings for each material it’s been a very enjoyable project with an awesome printer to use at the end.
