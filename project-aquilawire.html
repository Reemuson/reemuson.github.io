Aquilawire

"Aquilawire" is a heavily modified Voxelab Aquila X2 converted to CoreXZ akin to the Voron Switchwire.

It was bought for cheap on a black-friday sale and served me will with slight modification for many months. Until I discovered and fell into the world of Voron...

The switchwire provides linear rails, CoreXZ motion, kicking the Z screws to the curb, and saying goodbye to the hassle of dealing with z-wobble. It was like a laundry list of upgrades to boost speed, precision, and overall print quality.

I stumbled upon this gem of a project by boubounokefalos. They took the Aquila X2/Ender 3 frame and basically turned it into a Switchwire cousin. I printed the necessary parts & tweaked a few in ABS. I had to get everything just right. That marked the beginning of a whole new chapter.

But, of course, the modding journey doesn't stop there. Automatic z-offset became the next big thing on my list. Needed a nozzle end-stop for that, and I wasn't about to wait around for parts to ship. I fired up the lathe and turned out the needed parts.

I then came across the Klicky probe by jlas1 - a game-changer. This little guy became the key to hassle-free bed-levelling and nozzle swaps. Whipped up some configuration files in Klipper for auto-offset calibration. Now, I can switch nozzles easily, no feeler gauge required.

Now this printer takes it all in stride. Cruising at 200mm/s² speeds with 10k acceleration - it's a beast. Prints PLA, ABS, PETG, TPU. Albiet with lots of testing to find the right printer settings for each material it's been a very enjoyable project with an awesome printer to use at the end.
